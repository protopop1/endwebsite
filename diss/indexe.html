Introduction

Modern conveyor lines are characterised by their considerable length and the use of powerful drives. These are complex automation facilities, both in terms of controlling them and ensuring operational safety. The high efficiency of coal mining largely depends on the smooth and reliable operation of the conveyor transport, which is becoming increasingly important due to the distance of the mine faces from the cargo shafts.

Uneven mining traffic results in idle running of conveyors, unequal loading of conveyor lines and low efficiency of their use. According to observational data the factor of conveyors using by productivity makes nowadays KQ = 40-70 %, and the factor of conveyors using by time KW = 0,208-0,224. This adversely affects the technical and economic indicators of conveyors use: increase of specific power consumption, reduction of belt and rollers life time and, consequently, increase of coal transportation cost. To increase the coefficient of use of conveyors in terms of productivity it is necessary to stabilize the cargo flow coming to the conveyor belt.

1 Relevance of the theme

When emergencies occur in the work of the combine, process pauses and stoppages occur, which affect the feeding speed of the combine, and hence the miner's cargo flow is a random function in time. In order to correct the irregularity of the material flow and to obtain passport indicators of the conveyor performance, the load stabilisation unit BSPN, which is being developed, will be used.

Another problem is the loss of transported material, which affects the performance of the conveyor line. Tracking the amount of cargo on the belt will minimise the loss of transported material, eliminate the possibility of belt rupture, and consequently eliminate the downtime of the conveyor transport. Also the service life of the conveyor belt will be increased, due to obtaining of uniform cargo flow, which comes from the hopper. Due to the dispatching of the technological process, the operator will be able to control the modes of operation of the conveyor, see the address of the triggered sensor, as well as set the required value of the cargo flow.

2 Purpose and objectives of the study, planned results

The purpose of the master's work is to create safe operating conditions for conveyor transport and to ensure a stable load on the conveyor belt.

The object of automation is a belt conveyor with an accumulating hopper and conveyor scales.

A task of master's degree work is creation of the block of stabilisation of a linear load on the conveyor belt, by controlling position of the hopper gate, for maintenance of uniformity of a cargo flow on the conveyor belt.

The following main objectives of the study should be highlighted:

1 Analysis of the conveyor transport process as an object of automation.

2 Critical review of known technical solutions for automation of the process of conveyor transport and stabilisation of cargo flow.

3 Justification and development of the unit of stabilisation of the laden load on the conveyor belt.

3.1 Process analysis of mine conveyor transport as an automation object

Mine conveyor transport is designed for transporting coal and rock from the mine working and preparatory drifts to the mine working face, materials and equipment from the mine working face to the mine working (preparatory) faces and in the opposite direction, people to and from the workplace and filling materials.

The automation object is 1L800D conveyor, which is a part of transferring point.

Conveyor Belt Mine 1L800D - a single-drive conveyor, Figure 1, intended for transporting rock mass in a straight line in the mine workings of coal and shale mines.

The conveyor is the stationary installation of continuous action, traction-bearing, which device is the endless rubber-fibre belt supported on all extent by roller-frames and enveloping driving and turnover drums. The 1L800D belt conveyor as part of the transfer point is shown in figure 1.

Conveyor scales, figure 2, consist of two main assemblies: load holder and measuring system. The weighing device is mounted on the conveyor's stake (1).The weighing device consists of two support beams (2) and two suspended beams (3). 3.2 Overview of available technical solutions

As known control systems of conveyor transport, the automated system of control and management of conveyors and conveyor lines ASUK-DEP is used.

This system is intended for automated control of branched and not branched conveyor lines, and also single conveyors, included and not included in a conveyor line, in underground workings of mines and mines, and also in flow-transport systems of a surface complex.

The system has advanced self-diagnostics and self-testing facilities. The dispatcher receives information on equipment performance down to an individual module. The system has low power consumption - no more than 40 watts per conveyor set.

The structure of the main equipment of ACS-DEP includes: the central control station which is taking place in the dispatcher room and the block of control of the conveyor BUK (KS)-Ex (on one BUK on each conveyor). In addition, ACS-DEP can include: the control unit for slide gate BUC(S)-Ex, the control unit for hopper loading BUC(P)-Ex, the interface unit (bridge), the control panel for routes BUC(M)-Ex.

BUK(SH)-Ex -Ex - microprocessor control unit of the gate valve is made in explosion-proof version and is based on the multifunctional design-engineering complex Dekont-Ex. BUK(SH)-Ex controls the gates sensors and issues (based on the information received from the sensors) the corresponding control commands and information signals automatically (according to the programmed operation algorithm). The gate valve control unit BUK(S)-Ex is an auxiliary equipment and provides gate valve control either in a local mode - from the controls placed directly on the front panel of the unit or in a remote mode - from the dispatcher commands from the CPU. 3.3 Justification and development of circuit design. Algorithmisation of operation

When using the load stabilisation unit being developed, the transfer point should operate as follows. The conveyor operator or dispatcher at the operator/dispatcher station sets the parameters for the required conveyor capacity. This parameter is recorded by the conveyor scales and sent to the stabilisation block. The stabilisation block contains the PLC which processes the measured signal about quantity of a load on a conveyor belt and at its deviation from a set point, control of the gate. When the conveyor is loaded more than the set point, the gate is closed to a certain level until the load on the conveyor is equal to the set point. If the load falls below the set point, the opposite occurs.

Algorithm of operation of the load stabilisation block BSPN.

The process operation algorithm consists of the basic program of start and stop of the conveyor, and also the subprogram of the conveyor belt laden load control.

The load stabilisation block provides local and automated control of the conveyor and gate.

Local control is carried out by means of control buttons on the front panel of the operator's workstation.

In automated control, the conveyor and slide gate are controlled from the dispatcher/operator workstation. All monitored parameters are displayed on the dispatcher's PC in real time and all received data are archived.

1 The stabilisation unit operation process starts with initialisation of the automation system, i.e. assignment of all settings for triggering of sensors (without reintroduction upon system shutdown) belt run-off, overfeed, cable switch triggering, recording of set values (belt weight, belt speed). The load set points have their own range to reduce the number of gate drive on/off cycles.

2 The conveyor is then started by push-button, from the operator's or surface controller's station with a switch-on warning signal.

3 After switching on and waiting for the start-up time, the conveyor belt speed is initially checked to make sure that it is running at a speed Vfact≥0,75Vset. If the condition is not fulfilled, the conveyor is switched off and the information is transmitted to the operator and dispatcher.

4 When the speed condition is met, the control unit proceeds to the next point "Open gate" at half the maximum height, and then the "parameter control subprogram" is executed. In this subprogram the parameters from the sensors are continuously monitored.

The process of stabilisation of the load is as follows.

Signals from the weight, overflow sensor and speed sensors are fed to the PLC. The gate position is adjusted. If the actual load Qfact is within ±5% of Qset, normal operation will continue to be indicated. This is the main cycle of the programme. The subprogram is then exited and the condition is checked to see if there is an emergency or a request to stop the process. If there is none, the program returns to the parameter subprogram.

If there is a larger or smaller deviation from the set value, the gate position is adjusted and the load parameter is set back to the set value.

If the actual load exceeds the maximum permissible load, the operator/dispatcher station is signalled to exceed the load and the gate position is adjusted.

The Z=1 flag is set in case of an overload alarm and the Q=1 flag is set in case of an overload alarm.

When flag Z=1 is set, the time is counted down. If the overshoot signal time is more than 5s, the operator station will be signalled, the conveyor will be stopped and the slide gate will be closed.

When flag Q=1 is set, the time is counted down. If the belt overload signal is longer than 10 seconds, the operator's station will be signalled that the belt is overloaded. The slide gate is then closed to the minimum value without stopping the conveyor. After the actual load on the belt becomes less than 0.5Qmax, it will switch to sensor interrogation with subsequent regulation. If the signal duration is less than 10s, the subprogram will switch to the Sensor Interrogation Unit.

5 In case of an emergency situation, namely: belt speed exceeding, belt runaway, continuous maximum load on the belt, use of cable-rope switch or pressing the "STOP" button at the operator's or dispatcher's station, the transition to the "Process stop subprogram" block with subsequent signalling, conveyor shutdown and closing of the slide.

Conclusions

In this study:

- The conveyor transport of the mine as an object of automation has been considered;

- considered the work of reloading point;

- problems of irregularity of cargo traffic in mine were considered;

- the structural scheme of the developed block of stabilization of ladle-load BSPN is presented;

- an algorithm of the BSPN block operation was developed;

- the mathematical model of technological process is made, that has allowed to draw a conclusion: whether the developed block will really reach the set points.

Thus, as a result of mathematical modelling we received a transitive process of stabilisation of a cargo flow on the conveyor belt. Based on the graph, we can see that the calculated model reaches the specified load values when the PI controller is used.
